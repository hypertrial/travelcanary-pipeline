# Privacy notice

Effective: 2026-07-17.

This notice describes Hypertrial's handling of personal information associated
with the TravelCanary open-source project. It uses British Columbia's
[Personal Information Protection Act](https://www.bclaws.gov.bc.ca/civix/document/id/complete/statreg/00_03063_01)
as its baseline. It is an operational notice, not legal advice.

## Product data

TravelCanary is local-first software. It has no telemetry and sends no user,
warehouse, advisory, or usage data to Hypertrial. The software makes direct
requests from the operator's environment to the public sources documented in
`THIRD_PARTY_NOTICES.md`; those providers may independently log IP addresses,
request metadata, or other information under their own policies.

Do not put personal information, credentials, source payloads, or local
warehouse content in issues, pull requests, discussions, or security reports.

## Information Hypertrial may handle

Hypertrial may receive:

- public GitHub account names, commit authorship, DCO sign-offs, review
  comments, issue content, and other contribution metadata;
- an email address or other contact information when someone contacts
  `team@hypertrial.ai` or submits a private vulnerability report; and
- limited service metadata maintained by GitHub or an email provider.

Hypertrial collects, uses, and discloses this information only for purposes a
reasonable person would consider appropriate: operating and securing the
project, reviewing and attributing contributions, responding to requests,
enforcing project rules, meeting legal obligations, and maintaining an
accurate public record.

## Public contribution record and retention

Git commits, DCO sign-offs, issue and review text, names, account identifiers,
and other voluntarily submitted metadata are public. They may be copied by
GitHub users and retained indefinitely in repository history, mirrors, release
archives, and compliance records. Contributors should use a GitHub-provided
no-reply email address if they do not want a personal email address embedded
in public commits.

Private correspondence and security reports are retained only as long as
reasonably needed for response, security, legal, accounting, and audit
purposes, then deleted or de-identified where practical. Hypertrial may retain
information longer when required by law, needed to establish or defend legal
claims, or already embedded in an immutable public contribution record.

## Disclosure and service providers

Hypertrial does not sell personal information. It may disclose information to
service providers that host project collaboration or communications, to
professional advisers, or when authorized or required by law. Public
contribution metadata is disclosed worldwide by design through GitHub and
repository clones.

## Safeguards

Hypertrial uses safeguards appropriate to the sensitivity of the information,
including least-privilege repository access, protected branches, private
vulnerability reporting, secret scanning, and avoiding credentials or live
data in CI artifacts. No system can guarantee absolute security.

## Access, correction, withdrawal, and complaints

To ask what personal information Hypertrial controls about you, request a
correction, withdraw consent where applicable, or make a privacy complaint,
email `team@hypertrial.ai` with enough detail to locate the relevant record.
Hypertrial may verify identity and will respond within applicable legal
timeframes. Some requests cannot remove public contribution history or
information retained for legal reasons; Hypertrial will explain any refusal.

The designated privacy contact is:

> Privacy Lead, Hypertrial
> `team@hypertrial.ai`

If a concern is not resolved, an individual may contact the Office of the
Information and Privacy Commissioner for British Columbia.

## Changes

Hypertrial may update this notice as project practices or legal requirements
change. The effective date above identifies the current version.
